Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common questions

Who can donate blood?

Generally, donors must be at least 18 years old (16-17 with parental consent in some areas), weigh at least 110 pounds, and be in good health. Specific eligibility criteria may vary.

How often can I donate blood?

Whole blood donors can donate every 56 days (8 weeks). Platelet donors can donate more frequently - up to 24 times per year.

Is donating blood safe?

Yes, donating blood is safe. All equipment is sterile, used only once, and disposed of properly. Our staff are trained professionals who follow strict safety protocols.

How long does the donation process take?

The actual blood donation takes about 10-15 minutes. The entire process, including registration, health screening, and recovery, typically takes about 45-60 minutes.
